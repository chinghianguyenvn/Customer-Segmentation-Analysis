E-Commerce Customer Segmentation: K-Means & PCA AnalysisProject OverviewThis project focuses on effectively segmenting e-commerce customers based on demographic profiles, purchasing power, and lifestyle behaviors. By employing unsupervised machine learning, we identify distinct customer "personas" to help businesses transition from generic mass marketing to highly tailored, high-ROI marketing strategies.Research Question"How can companies effectively segment e-commerce customers to tailor personalized marketing strategies?" MethodologyThe study utilizes a two-step quantitative approach:K-means Clustering: Used to segment users by minimizing the within-cluster sum of squares (WCSS) to ensure cohesive grouping.Principal Component Analysis (PCA): Applied to reduce dimensionality and visualize the multivariate continuous data in a 2D feature space.The analysis was performed in R, utilizing key libraries such as ggplot2 and dplyr.Key Customer PersonasThe model identified four highly distinctive segments:Wealthy Switchers: High-income users (Avg. $157k) with the lowest brand loyalty (2.4).Young Loyal Advocates: The youngest demographic (Avg. age 31.8) with the highest brand loyalty (7.59) and high impulse buying scores.Senior Loyalists: The oldest demographic (Avg. age 66.3) with stable, high loyalty scores (7.58).Budget-Conscious Explorers: Price-sensitive users with the lowest average income ($52k) and limited brand loyalty.Strategic ImplicationsTo optimize Return on Investment (ROI), the following interventions are recommended:VIP Exclusivity: For Wealthy Switchers, focus on premium services to create "psychological switching costs".Conversion Optimization: For Young Loyal Advocates, optimize UI/UX for 1-click checkouts and mobile flash sales.Accessibility & Support: For Senior Loyalists, prioritize simple UI/UX and traditional customer support.Price Stimulus: For Budget-Conscious Explorers, allocate the bulk of the discount and bundle deal budget.Visualizing the ClustersThe project includes a 2D Customer Segmentation Map created through PCA, which demonstrates the clear separation between the identified personas .
